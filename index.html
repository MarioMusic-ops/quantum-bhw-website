Psychiatric Nurse Practitioner

Abidemi “Abby” Adewoyin

APRN, RN, PMHNP-BC

Abidemi “Abby” Adewoyin is a board-certified Psychiatric-Mental Health Nurse Practitioner who provides thoughtful, evidence-based psychiatric evaluations and medication management. She helps clients better understand their symptoms, treatment options, and medication plans in a clear, supportive, and judgment-free environment. Abby is passionate about making mental health care more accessible, inclusive, and culturally responsive for individuals from underserved and marginalized communities.

  • Clinical focus: Psychiatric evaluations, diagnosis, medication management, treatment planning, symptom monitoring, and support for clients exploring whether medication may help improve their mental health
  • Specialties: Depression, generalized anxiety, social anxiety, panic symptoms, ADHD, bipolar disorder, PTSD, trauma- and stressor-related conditions, mood disorders, behavioral health concerns, and medication adjustment for clients who have tried multiple medications without success
  • Populations served: Young adults, adults, seniors, individuals, Black and Brown communities, LGBTQ+ clients, immigrant patients, underserved communities, and clients seeking culturally responsive psychiatric care
  • Approach: Warm, collaborative, evidence-based, trauma-informed, culturally responsive, and judgment-free. Abby explains medication options in plain language, answers questions openly, and partners with clients to create treatment plans that feel safe, informed, and aligned with their goals

Dana Humphrey

She supports clients navigating complex mental health needs, substance use concerns, major life transitions, and psychosocial barriers. Dana creates a welcoming and collaborative space where clients feel heard, respected, and empowered to build resilience, develop practical coping strategies, and move toward healing and personal growth.

  • Clinical focus: Complex mental health needs, substance use concerns, major life transitions, stress management, trauma-informed support, psychosocial barriers, treatment planning, and community resource navigation
  • Specialties: Substance use and recovery support, anxiety, stress, emotional challenges, family systems, narrative therapy, relational concerns, strengths-based care, motivational interviewing, solution-focused support, and resilience-building
  • Populations served: Teenagers, young adults, adults, seniors, individuals, veterans, clients experiencing homelessness or housing instability, individuals navigating recovery, underserved communities, and clients facing major life transitions
  • Approach: Compassionate, client-centered, collaborative, culturally sensitive, and strengths-based. Dana integrates evidence-based approaches such as ACT, CBT, solution-focused therapy, motivational interviewing, compassion-focused care, family systems, narrative therapy, relational therapy, and structural family therapy to help clients build practical tools for healing, growth, and long-term well-being

Dr. Cal E. Spinks is a Licensed Professional Counselor with over two decades of experience providing compassionate, culturally responsive mental health care. His work centers on multicultural counseling, trauma recovery, and support for individuals and communities that have historically been marginalized or underserved.   • Specialties: Trauma-informed care, LGBTQ+ counseling, culturally responsive therapy, empowerment and advocacy, mindfulness-based support, cognitive-behavioral strategies, attachment-based work, and compassion-focused care

Common Questions,
Clear Next Steps

Do you accept insurance?

Yes. Insurance is accepted. Specific plans should be confirmed through the Client Interest Form or during consultation.

Do you offer telehealth?

Yes. Telehealth is available throughout Missouri, Illinois, Texas, and beyond where appropriate and permitted.

What states do you serve?

Quantum Behavioral Health & Wellness serves Missouri, Illinois, Texas, and additional clients through telehealth when appropriate.

How much does therapy cost?

Cost can vary based on insurance, service type, clinician, and appointment format. The team can confirm details after the interest form is submitted.

How do I schedule?

Use the 'Get Started' button, complete the Client Interest Form, or call 888-441-3959.

What happens during my first appointment?

Your first appointment helps the team understand your needs, answer questions, and identify the best next step.

What if I’m not sure therapy is right for me?

That is okay. The consultation process can help determine whether therapy, medication services, group counseling, or another resource is the best fit.

How do I request medication services?

Complete the Client Interest Form and select medication management or psychiatric evaluation as your service of interest.

Do you provide services for children?

Yes. Child and adolescent counseling may support ADHD, school concerns, social skills, and emotional regulation.

How do I join a group?

Current and upcoming groups can be listed under Group Counseling and Resources. You can also ask through the Client Interest Form.
