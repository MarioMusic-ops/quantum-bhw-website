Meet Dr. Cal

Dr. Calvin “Dr. Cal” Spinks brings more than 30 years of advocacy, mentorship, teaching, and clinical leadership to communities that have too often been unseen, unheard, or underserved.

His work is grounded in resilience, cultural awareness, whole-person care, and the belief that every person deserves to be seen, valued, and supported in the fullness of who they are.

His Story

A life’s work rooted in resilience, advocacy, and transformation.

Dr. Cal’s journey is one of perseverance and purpose. Having known struggle personally and professionally, he brings a deep understanding of what it means to move through adversity, rebuild, and rise with clarity and strength.

Born in the inner-city projects, Dr. Cal’s story reflects the realities many individuals and families face: emotional hardship, physical challenges, social barriers, and systems that do not always make healing accessible. Rather than allowing those experiences to define him, he transformed them into a foundation for advocacy and service.

With advanced training in sociology, counseling, counselor education, and supervision, Dr. Cal combines academic preparation with lived experience. His approach honors the whole person: their culture, history, identity, environment, and personal story.

For communities of color and other historically underserved groups, healing is often layered. It can be personal, cultural, generational, and systemic. Dr. Cal’s work recognizes that therapy and wellness must make space for all of those realities.

Healing is not one-size-fits-all.

Dr. Cal believes that meaningful care begins with presence, cultural awareness, and deep respect for each client’s story. His work challenges the idea that people should have to shrink themselves to receive support. Instead, he helps create space where people can be fully seen, fully heard, and fully human.

Reflections from clients, students, and colleagues.

These testimonials reflect Dr. Cal’s impact as a clinician, educator, mentor, and advocate.

“

I started seeing Dr. Cal the summer of the pandemic. When I came to Dr. Cal, I had already experienced a wealth of disappointment. I was severely depressed and had been for a while. Our sessions always started with roses and thorns. I probably had more thorns than roses.

Dr. Cal spent a great deal of time coaching and teaching me to deal with and handle my feelings, as I had put them away for the sake of others. Eventually, I realized it was okay to think about me, my needs, and what the future looked like.

Through all of my sessions with Dr. Cal’s help, here I am today happy with life and excited about my future. The vision boards, self-help books, and exercises I learned with Dr. Cal’s help have helped me become a better person for myself. I now advocate for myself in areas I always found stifling.

I was at a planning dinner with seven friends. We all laughed throughout the evening. Then one friend said, “I’m not sure who the Kevin was I met two years ago, but I absolutely love this Kevin.” This process has been the most fulfilling and life-changing.

Kevin Washington, D.C.
